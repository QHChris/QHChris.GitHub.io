Forensic      Lab

The Forensic Lab was the gruesome station… so was absolutely intriguing to the children!  The aim of the station was to suggest that Jesus was unlikely to have swooned, recovered and then moved the stone himself.  The children look at the injuries Jesus sustained and are then asked to think about how much blood he might have lost, the effects of crucifixion, the professionalism of the soldiers that pronounced him dead and the effect of lying on a cold slab for two nights and a day.


This is achieved by showing them a Roman flagrum (whip), 6” nails, a spear and a crown of thorns and seeing what 2 litres of “blood” looks like.  Flagrum, nails and spear we had as props, the crown of thorns was made - we found black cable ties work very well to hold it together (as the thorns were very sharp we included a safety notice and a pair of leather gloves to hold it…. which all added to the effect) - and the “blood” was made from a thick gravy mixed with red food colouring.


The suffocating effects of crucifixion were experienced by inviting each child to suspend themselves from a frame we made and hung from beams - as you can imagine this they loved!

The effect was completed by flicking plenty of red paint across a white paper table cloth.
