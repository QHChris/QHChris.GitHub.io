‘Who moved the stone?’, has been written for use with Key Stage 2 (Junior) classes. It was developed by St. Luke’s Huntington and St. Paul’s, Boughton in Chester as a follow up to ‘Experience Easter’. Essentially the children are invited to play detective as they worked their way through five stations looking at whether anything other than the New Testament description of supernatural resurrection could possibly have happened to Jesus between Good Friday afternoon and Easter Sunday morning.


Each child was given a booklet containing a timeline of events and a series of questions for each station aimed at helping them to observe facts and then think about their implications. For every question there were bright yellow CSI-style evidence markers directing them to the information required. There was also an adult helper at each station to assist the children. The event started with a welcome/ introduction (including showing the children how the booklets and evidence markers worked) and ended with a plenary session.
