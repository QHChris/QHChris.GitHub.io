The Museum station aims to address the question, “But was Jesus real or can we just dismiss it all as a fairy story?”  The aim is to show that there is some surprisingly stark evidence both from the New Testament and from other sources pointing towards Jesus as an historic figure.


However, this station proved complex and required the most adult assistance.  It started off with a discussion around eye witnesses, how they would agree in general but may disagree on points of detail because of their different perspectives.  The Gospels are then introduced as eye witness accounts of Jesus’ life.  This (opposite) short clip from the Alpha Youth Film Series was then used to show that the reliability of the New Testament documents far exceeds

the reliability of any other ancient documents (such as everything we know about the life of Julius Caesar) that we all take at face value and assume to be true.

The Museum exhibits included:

  • texts from Josephus and Pliny (as sources external to the New Testament that also point to Jesus);
  • full sized copies of the John Rylands fragment from John’s Gospel (dated 125 AD), which is double sided, so was cut out and laminated, and a page from the Chester Beatty Codex (dated 250 AD), also cut out and laminated with its yellow evidence marker;
  • Printed details about the James Ossuary and the Pilate Stone along with a full sized cardboard version of the ossuary as archaeological evidence pointing to Jesus.

There wasn’t always time for much interaction with the museum exhibits after the discussion and the video, especially for the younger classes, but some of the older classes engaged well with it, particularly when a doctor, who helped run the station, brought in a human thigh bone to show how it would fit into the box!  The printouts for this station can be found here.
