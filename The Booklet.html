The booklet is absolutely key to the success of the event.  Each child was given a copy along with a pencil and clipboard (we asked the schools to bring along clipboards).  The front cover uses a very old CPO image and can easily be amended to change church names etc.  The back cover we used to advertise our other Easter events.  Inside the front page we included two timelines.  The first to place Jesus within the scope of the history topics they might have studied at school and the second to recap the main events of the first Easter for them.  Both of these timelines were highlighted during the introduction.



The remainder of the booklet included observation questions and “for thought” questions for each station.  Each of the observation questions is linked by a number to one of the bright yellow evidence markers that we used at every station (except the Police HQ).  This made it very easy for the children to find the particular piece of evidence that went with each question.



The adult at each station had a copy of the booklet and led the children through the points they were looking for and helped them with the “for thought” questions.  We tried very hard not to direct the children towards the correct answer as we felt that self-discovery for those that did get there would have a far more profound impact.  

The inside page we photocopied ourselves but the outside page was A4, double-sided, medium weight, unlaminated printed here.  We then had a production line after church one Sunday to put the booklets together.  A pdf of our final version is here (outside colour pages) and here (inside black and white pages).  An editable version of the outside colour pages is here (in Open Office format, if you have it!).
